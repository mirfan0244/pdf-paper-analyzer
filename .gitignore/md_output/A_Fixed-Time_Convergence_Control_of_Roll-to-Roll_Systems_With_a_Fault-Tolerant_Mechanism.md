This paper presents research on advancing roll-to-roll manufacturing systems by addressing disturbances and faults through a novel fixed-time convergence control approach. Conducted primarily by Nguyen Hien Thi et al., the 2025 study focuses on enhancing system productivity and stability in such industrial setups, particularly when external factors or actuator failures influence performance.

Notably, this research proposes integrating Fixed-Time Sliding Mode Control within a Backstepping framework. This method aims at ensuring fast fault compensation while maintaining desired tensions for quality production. The research uniquely combines an observer design for estimating system disturbances and faults with fixed-time convergence properties, leading to improved robustness and reliability in high-precision applications.

The study is distinguished by its application of mathematical rigor to demonstrate fixed-time stability of the control strategy using Lyapunov theory. Through both simulation and theoretical analysis, this approach shows significant improvements over existing methods across different operational conditions such as disturbances and actuator faults, underlining its practical relevance for advanced manufacturing technologies.
