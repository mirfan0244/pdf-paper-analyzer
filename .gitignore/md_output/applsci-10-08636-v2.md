The paper "Web Unevenness Due to Thermal Deformation in the Roll-to-Roll Manufacturing Process" examines how thermal deformation impacts web uniformity and coating quality during roll-to-roll manufacturing. The research employs finite-element analysis to study thermal and mechanical deformations across different drying temperatures, identifying that higher temperatures exacerbate web unevenness. This results in defects such as cracks in coatings like yttria-stabilized zirconia, which is utilized for solid oxide fuel cells (SOFCs). Experimental analysis confirmed more significant cracking and imperfections at elevated drying temperatures, thereby impairing device performance. The study underscores the necessity of considering these aspects to optimize manufacturing processes for high-quality large-area SOFC production.
