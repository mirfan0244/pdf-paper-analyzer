**Markdown Summary**

The report titled *Economic Analysis of Technology Infrastructure Needs for Advanced Manufacturing: Roll-to-Roll Manufacturing* explores the current resurgence in roll-to-roll (R2R) manufacturing methods. These continuous production techniques are gaining interest due to their capacity to produce complex, multilayered products efficiently. The study was carried out by Alan C. O’Connor, Travis J. Beaulieu, and Ginger D. Rothrock in 2016 under the auspices of RTI International for the U.S. Department of Commerce's Economic Analysis Office.

Significant technical challenges are identified as impediments to the development and adoption of advanced R2R manufacturing. These include the need for new standards, improved metrology techniques, innovative materials, and enhanced tooling technologies. Through interviews with industry experts, academia, and a comprehensive literature review, the report highlights these barriers.

The economic analysis included in this study estimates that addressing technology infrastructure needs could yield an annual economic benefit of no less than $353 million. This figure, regarded as conservative, reflects the potential impacts on reducing costs, increasing productivity, and enabling new products to enter the market. The authors underscore the role crucial public-private partnerships can play in fostering technology adoption by overcoming barriers associated with high costs and long learning curves.

The findings aim to aid NIST's strategic planning concerning infrastructure needs for advanced manufacturing sectors, specifically focusing on how investments in technology could enhance competitive edge, reduce overall costs, improve product quality, and accelerate the arrival of innovative products.
