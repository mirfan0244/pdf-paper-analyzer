# Summary

This paper presents an experimental validation of high-precision web handling for a two-actuator-based roll-to-roll (R2R) system. Developed by a team with the primary affiliation at Korea Institute of Machinery and Materials, it explores advanced control strategies to manage the transportation of flexible electronics within such systems.

Key to this study is the utilization of tension and velocity control loops managed via cascade control strategies—one governing the unwinder module for tension regulation and the other managing the rewinder's web speed. These processes are refined through a radius estimation algorithm that enhances accuracy in maintaining consistent velocities during material transport phases, especially crucial when varying roller sizes.

The experimental findings demonstrate notable precision, achieving less than 2% error margin in both tension tracking and web speed control across different operational speeds—0.1 m/s, 0.2 m/s, and 0.3 m/s. Further validation was conducted by altering tension profiles, confirming the system's robustness.

The research contributes significantly to R2R systems' development, offering insights into effectively handling flexible materials in high-speed production environments while maintaining high quality and reliability as underscored within disciplines of sensors and manufacturing automation.
