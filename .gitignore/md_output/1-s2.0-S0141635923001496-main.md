# Summary

This study from **2023** explores a new method to prevent axial slip in center-wound rolls through an advanced winding tension strategy. Introduced by Dehui Wu and Shibin Lin, the work presents both a theoretical model and a practical neural network-based approach to dynamically adjust tensions as rolls wind.

The central issue addressed is the axial slip during the winding process, which degrades roll quality significantly. By analyzing mechanical relationships between layers of the center-wound roll, the study develops a mathematical model correlating axial slip with winding tension.

A special neural network structure with backpropagation training dynamically updates the winding tension according to changes in the rolling radius. The practical application of this method is verified through experiments involving a roll-to-roll slitting system for lithium-ion battery components. These tests confirm that the optimized winding tension eliminates axial slip issues, indicating a successful strategy for improving wound roll quality.

The paper adds valuable insights into the precision engineering field by advancing our understanding and control over potential defects during rolling processes, particularly applicable in contexts like flexible electronics and battery manufacturing.
