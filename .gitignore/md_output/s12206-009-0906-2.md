The paper titled "Effect of Taper Tension Profile on Telescoping in a Winding Process of High-Speed Roll to Roll Printing Systems" published in 2009, by authors from the Flexible Display Roll to Roll Research Center and the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Konkuk University. It falls under the research field of Mechanical Science and Technology. The study develops mathematical models correlating taper tension profiles with telescoping in roll-to-roll systems. Emphasis is on both linear and hybrid taper tensions, taking into account nip-induced tension. Methodologically, it combines simulation with experimental verification using roll-to-roll printing systems. Key findings indicate the superiority of linear taper profiles in minimizing telescoping defects compared to other tension types. The paper contributes to optimizing control strategies for print winding processes, important for print quality maintenance.
