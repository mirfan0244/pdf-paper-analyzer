# Markdown Summary

This research addresses control challenges in multi-shaft systems with web materials linkage, essential for industries like flexible displays, solar cells, and lighting. The paper proposes adaptive control mechanisms integrating fuzzy disturbance observers and high-gain disturbance observers within a sliding mode control framework to manage the system's imprecision and external disturbances.

### Main Contributions

1. **Model Development**: A multi-shaft linkage model is introduced considering variable material inertia, crucial for accurate tension and velocity outputs in web transport systems.
   
2. **Adaptive Control Strategy**: An innovative adaptive fuzzy algorithm coupled with a high-gain disturbance observer improves control accuracy by compensating disturbances without relying heavily on precise system models.

3. **Performance Evaluation**: Utilizing Lyapunov’s stability theory, the tracking error's convergence properties are verified through numerical results, demonstrating superior performance compared to traditional methods in maintaining system outputs under uncertainties and disturbances.

The paper is structured to first describe the model of multi-shaft linkage systems, followed by the design of sliding mode control, adaptive fuzzy algorithms, and high-gain disturbance observer. The concluding section presents simulation validations, emphasizing the controllers' robust responses against operational variances. Overall, this study contributes significantly to advancing control systems for complex industrial applications.
