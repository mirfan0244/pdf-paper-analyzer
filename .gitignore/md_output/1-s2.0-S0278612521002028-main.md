# Summary

This research paper presents a new control method for low-tension roll-to-roll (R2R) systems used in manufacturing low-strength sheets. Traditional tension-based feedback controllers can crack these sheets; therefore, the authors propose an innovative sag-based feedback approach where sags act as the primary control metric.

The proposed multi-roll coordinating optimal control adjusts roll speeds to stabilize the system when upstream velocity changes. The study introduces a MIMO (multiple-input multiple-output) linear-quadratic-integral controller for enhanced performance, which is validated through extensive simulations and real-world testing. Results demonstrate that this method significantly reduces speed fluctuations and improves overall system stability compared to conventional single-input single-output PID control methods.

Overall, the paper addresses critical manufacturing issues by enhancing R2R systems' efficiency in producing low-strength materials while minimizing production waste and cost.
