The research presents a novel framework for decision-making in predictive maintenance enhanced through digital twin technology. By integrating various technologies like data sharing and resource management, this framework aims to rectify existing limitations faced in typical maintenance regimes focusing mainly on fault diagnosis and equipment health predictions within an uncertain parameter space.

Implemented within a manufacturing context, the study develops a mathematical programming model incorporating an NSGA-II hybrid algorithm, which efficiently addresses uncertainty parameters. The integration of digital twin facilitates heightened accuracy in fault diagnostics and supports more reliable maintenance planning through real-time data synchronization across physical and virtual spaces.

An emphasis on dynamic adjustment strategies is discussed to accommodate changing conditions within actual operational environments, ensuring responsive and adaptable maintenance processes. The utility and advantages of the proposed framework are illustrated with a practical example involving the predictive maintenance decision-making for bearings used in large vertical mills, demonstrating significant improvements over existing approaches. This work underscores the potential of digital twin technology in advancing intelligent predictive maintenance strategies in industrial applications.
