## Summary

The paper presents a novel roll-to-roll electrodeposition and transfer-printing method for prelithiating lithium-ion battery anodes, particularly addressing the need for high initial Coulombic efficiencies (ICE) in batteries. The developed system enables continuous production compatible with industrial processes, improving both efficiency and scalability. Key highlights include achieving ICEs of 99.99% and 99.05% in graphite and silicon/carbon composite electrodes, respectively. This advancement contributes significantly to overcoming limitations like energy density and capacity loss prevalent in conventional lithium-ion batteries. The paper emphasizes the potential for broader application with its adaptable manufacturing technique, aiming for widespread commercial use, especially in electric vehicles.
