# Summary
The paper presents advancements in developing organic-based secondary batteries using roll-to-roll printing techniques for printed electronic devices. The study focuses on creating a fully printed battery with an open-circuit voltage (OCV) greater than 2.0 V by employing poly(1,5-anthraquinonyl sulfide) as the anode and LiMn2O4 without Co or Ni in the cathode. The research demonstrates successful assembly under ambient conditions with a lifespan of over 100 cycles at significant capacities. In addition to lithium-ion batteries, the study extends these findings to sodium-ion batteries using PAQS electrodes and achieves noteworthy capacities. This work aims to leverage environmentally friendly materials for scalable and cost-effective battery production, targeting smart packaging applications and small IoT devices within printed electronics.
