The research paper titled "Taper Tension Profile in Roll‑to‑Roll Rewinder: Improving Adhesive Force of Pressure-Sensitive Adhesive Film" examines the impact of winding tension on the quality of pressure-sensitive adhesive (PSA) films manufactured using roll-to-roll processes. The study conducted by Jongsu Lee, Seongyong Kim, and Changwoo Lee in 2019 explores how hoop and radial stresses within wound rolls affect PSA film's adhesive force. Employing a combination of theoretical modeling and experimental verification, the researchers developed different taper tension profiles (linear and hyperbolic) to analyze their effects on reducing wear-related issues in adhesive layers.

The findings demonstrate that altering winding tension profiles can improve the quality of adhesive films without modifying other production factors such as film properties or environmental conditions. By effectively managing internal stresses, the study concludes that optimizing taper tension profiles enhances the intrinsic adhesive capabilities of PSA films, thereby suggesting a significant methodological advancement for manufacturing processes reliant on roll-to-roll technologies.
