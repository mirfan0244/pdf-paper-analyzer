## Summary

This research paper explores how artificial intelligence (AI), particularly Bayesian optimization algorithms, can enhance the cost efficiency of developing semiconductor process technologies. The study emphasizes a collaborative model where human engineers and AI work in tandem—a strategy termed as Human First–Computer Last (HF-CL)—to lower the development costs significantly.

Human engineers excel in early-stage development due to their domain knowledge, while computer algorithms perform better when refining processes to meet exact target tolerances. By leveraging both domains' strengths at different stages of the process, the overall cost efficiency is improved compared to relying solely on either human expertise or machine learning alone. The study was conducted using a controlled virtual environment that mirrors real-world semiconductor fabrication processes, allowing for rigorous and systematic comparison without the high variability of physical experiments.

The results indicate that initially, human experts should guide AI algorithms by providing them with essential data points from their experience until a transfer point is achieved. Past this point, computer algorithms can autonomously continue the process refinement more efficiently. The success of this hybrid strategy provides valuable insights for integrating AI in semiconductor manufacturing and potentially other fields facing similar engineering challenges. Furthermore, it highlights cultural shifts required to integrate human intuition with algorithmic precision effectively. Overall, the paper presents a groundbreaking approach to reducing costs in developing complex semiconductor technologies through successful collaboration between humans and machines.
