The paper titled "Hybrid Device Fabrication Using Roll-to-Roll Printing for Personal Environmental Monitoring" outlines a method where hybrid electronic devices are fabricated using roll-to-roll (R2R) screen-printing. These devices consist of multilayer circuits with polymer insulating and conductive layers, assembled together with solid-state components. The study addresses issues related to the efficiency of material processing and pattern alignment during R2R printing by proposing compensation techniques for better device quality. Importantly, these hybrid electronic devices are utilized in a personal environmental monitoring system that can collect data via mobile phones, process it through IoT connections, and upload results to cloud servers. This approach highlights applications in real-time environmental monitoring, crucial for public health and sustainable development.
